High feed prices will cause theChinese to reduce hog herd growth and pork production thisyear, the U.S. Agriculture Department said.    In its World Production and Trade Report, the departmentsaid hog numbers at the start of 1987 were estimated at 331.6mln head, up slightly from 1986, and 10 mln head above earlierprojections for 1987.    Pork production in 1986 was up 4.2 pct to 17.25 mln tonnes,slightly below earlier estimates, it said.    For 1987, production is projected to fall to 17.05 mlntonnes.    Feed prices at the end of January were reported 35 to 40pct above year-ago levels, the department said. Reuter&#3;
